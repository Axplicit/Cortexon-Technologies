EICR Certification in Glasgow

Welcome to WES Electrical! As a trusted provider of Electrical Installation Condition Reports (EICR) in Glasgow, we aim to keep you informed about the latest legislation and the importance of EICR testing for your property

What is EICR Testing?

An EICR Certificate, or Electrical Installation Condition Report testing, involves a comprehensive assessment of the electrical installations within a property. This inspection ensures that all electrical systems are safe, compliant with current standards, and functioning correctly. An EICR identifies any potential hazards, deterioration, defects, or non-compliances with the electrical regulations.

New Legislation in Glasgow

Recent changes in legislation have made EICR testing a legal requirement for landlords and property owners in Glasgow and throughout Scotland. The new regulations are designed to enhance the safety of tenants and reduce the risk of electrical accidents, fires, and fatalities.

Key Points of the New Legislation:

  1. Mandatory EICR Testing for Landlords: All landlords must ensure their properties have a valid EICR certificate, carried out by a qualified electrician, at least every five years or at the beginning of a new tenancy.
  2. Compliance with Standards: The inspection must comply with the 18th Edition of the Wiring Regulations (BS 7671:2018), ensuring the highest safety standards are met.
  3. Remedial Works: If the EICR identifies any 'Code 1' (immediate danger) or 'Code 2' (potential danger) issues, landlords are required to carry out remedial works within 28 days to rectify these issues and provide tenants with written confirmation that the work has been completed.
  4. Penalties for Non-Compliance: Landlords who fail to comply with the EICR testing requirements can face significant fines and legal action, emphasizing the importance of adhering to the new rules.

Why EICR Testing is Essential

EICR testing is crucial for ensuring the safety of electrical installations in both residential and commercial properties. Regular inspections can:

  1. Prevent Electrical Hazards: Identify and rectify potential electrical hazards before they become serious risks.
  2. Ensure Compliance: Meet legal obligations and avoid penalties for non-compliance with electrical safety regulations.
  3. Protect Tenants: Provide a safe living environment for tenants, reducing the risk of electrical accidents and improving overall safety.
  4. Maintain Property Value: Keep electrical systems in good condition, maintaining the value and integrity of the property.

EICR Certificates Glasgow

After the inspection, you will receive an EICR certificate, detailing the condition of your electrical installations. The certificate will include:

  • Inspection Details: Date of inspection, name, and qualifications of the electrician.
  • Findings: A summary of the inspection results, including any issues identified and their severity.
  • Recommendations: Suggestions for remedial actions or improvements needed to ensure compliance with safety standards.
  • Certification: Confirmation that the property meets the required electrical safety standards or a list of necessary actions to achieve compliance.
